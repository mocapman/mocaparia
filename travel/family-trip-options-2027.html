Maui vs New Orleans vs San Diego

What the trip needs to do

Group profile

  • Food matters a lot
  • Music matters a lot
  • Walking is good, but not hard-core all day
  • Some want sun, water, hiking, dancing, and more activity
  • Some want easy support roles, good meals, and good atmosphere
  • The destination should allow the group to split naturally without breaking the trip

Best operating model

Most realistic structure: Dave & Lilli cover the shared lodging. Everyone else likely covers their own airfare. That keeps the gift meaningful without turning the planning into a seven-ticket award-booking project.
  • Best lodging style: one large house / condo / villa
  • Ground transportation goal: one minivan if possible, two vehicles only if necessary
  • Ideal trip length: 4-6 nights for New Orleans, 5-7 nights for San Diego or Maui

Airfare snapshot to help frame interest

These are directional notes, not book-now quotes. 2027 flights are not bookable yet. This is based on current Google Flights route pages and a few fare aggregators to see whether SFO or LAX looks materially cheaper for Maui and New Orleans. Bottom line: LAX is a little stronger for New Orleans, but SFO and LAX look fairly close for Maui.

LAX → Maui (OGG) Google Flights route pages show fares starting around $335, with typical ranges often listed roughly in the mid $300s to $700s+ depending on season and carrier. LAX may be a touch better on some dates, but not enough to make Maui an obvious LAX-only play.
SFO → New Orleans (MSY) Google Flights and other fare pages generally show low-end roundtrips from around $180-$210, with nonstops often higher. SFO is workable, but not the cheapest path.
LAX → New Orleans (MSY) Google Flights and other fare pages generally show low-end roundtrips from around $160-$185, with nonstop options often looking better than SFO. LAX has a modest edge for New Orleans, especially if someone wants a cleaner nonstop.

April lens

  • Maui: strong weather, warmer/drier season, usually easier to sell as a premium trip
  • New Orleans: weather is much better than summer, but April can collide with major festivals and price spikes
  • San Diego: mild, comfortable, easy for walking and mixed activities

August lens

  • Maui: very strong beach and pool weather, but hotter and busier
  • New Orleans: this is the weakest weather case - hot, humid, rainy, more indoor planning needed
  • San Diego: probably the easiest summer choice with reliable coast weather and beach payoff
Emotionally reliable pick

🌺 Maui

Core idea: The safest “this will probably work” destination. Not new, but proven. The house/condo itself becomes part of the gift, and the island naturally supports split agendas.

Why Maui fits this family

  • Strong for beach, pool, sun, scenic drives, good food, casual nightlife, and active day trips
  • Easy for people to do different things without feeling like the group broke apart
  • Works especially well if the goal is togetherness more than novelty

Pros

  • Very high memory value
  • Easy to picture a shared house / condo rhythm
  • Great for Lilli, Tiago, Stephany, Shawn, and anyone who wants sun/water/activity
  • Still good for food, drives, scenic support role, and flexible pacing
  • April and August both work

Cons

  • Longest travel day of the three
  • Most expensive destination once airfare + lodging + rental vehicles stack up
  • Not a new destination for the family
  • Requires rental-car planning early if you want flexibility
  • Some of the destination’s biggest strengths are exactly the parts Dave personally does not care about

April vs August

  • April: better shoulder-season feel, great weather, easier case for mixed group comfort
  • August: hotter, stronger beach/pool weather, good summer energy, but can feel more expensive and busier

Ground logistics

  • Most likely: one minivan plus maybe a second car if the house is far from activity clusters
  • Possible strategy: stay in one strong base and reduce cross-island bouncing

Best personality match

🎺 New Orleans

Core idea: The city that feels most like this family’s personality - food, music, walking, culture, late nights, split-off exploration, and memorable group meals.

Why New Orleans fits this family

  • Probably the strongest food + music destination of the three
  • Great for a social group that likes to wander, eat, listen, and regroup
  • Can work especially well if the trip is shorter and denser, like 4-5 nights

Pros

  • Best destination personality fit
  • High cultural payoff relative to airfare cost
  • Walkability can reduce rental-car stress if you stay in the right area
  • Very strong for group dinners, music nights, neighborhood wandering, and memorable atmosphere
  • LAX looks a bit better than SFO on airfare

Cons

  • Weakest sun/water destination of the three
  • August weather is a real negative - hot, humid, rainy
  • The city’s drinking culture is embedded in the experience, which matters if Rock joins while still protecting sobriety
  • Less “shared house paradise” energy than Maui
  • April can get expensive and crowded around major festival periods

April vs August

  • April: strongest seasonal case, but dates matter because festival timing can spike costs and crowds
  • August: weakest weather of the three destinations by far

Ground logistics

  • Best case: stay central and avoid renting a van for every day
  • Use walking, rideshare, and streetcars for most of the trip

Best brain / value pick

🌴 San Diego

Core idea: The most rational compromise. Less dramatic than Maui, less culturally singular than New Orleans, but maybe the best balance of ease, price, weather, beach access, food, neighborhoods, and family flexibility.

Why San Diego fits this family

  • Beach people still get beach
  • Food and walk people still get neighborhoods and good meals
  • Active people can add hikes, coast walks, paddle, climbing, or day trips

Pros

  • Probably best bang for the buck
  • Easier and cheaper than Maui
  • Friendlier weather than New Orleans in August
  • Flexible trip design - beach, food, neighborhoods, some nightlife, some activity
  • Easy to imagine a big coastal house or a central base plus simpler daily logistics

Cons

  • May feel less special than Maui
  • May feel less distinctive than New Orleans
  • Can become “nice California trip” instead of “big family memory” if the base is generic
  • You need to choose the right zone - La Jolla, Coronado, Mission/Pacific Beach area, or a strong central base

April vs August

  • April: excellent for walking, general comfort, mixed activities
  • August: strongest beach payoff, easiest summer weather case of the three

Ground logistics

  • Most likely: one minivan works if the group stays reasonably coordinated
  • Can reduce driving if based near walkable neighborhoods and beach access

Bottom line

Maui = heart

If the goal is the most emotionally reliable family memory, Maui is still the strongest pick. It costs more and takes more travel energy, but it gives the group the widest range of payoff.

New Orleans = soul

If the goal is to pick the destination that most feels like this family’s personality, New Orleans may be the best match, especially in April and especially for a shorter trip.

San Diego = brain

If the goal is best value with the fewest regrets, San Diego may be the smartest overall choice. It gives a lot without asking everyone to pay Maui-level airfare or accept New Orleans summer weather.

Simple recommendation by season

  • April: New Orleans or Maui
  • August: San Diego or Maui

If New Orleans stays on the list, April is the season that gives it the fairest fight.
