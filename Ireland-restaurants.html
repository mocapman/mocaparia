Dublin · May 5–7

Best fit near Travelodge PLUS Dublin City Centre. Strong Irish comfort food, central, and actually useful for a first-night or full-day city dinner.

The Woollen Mills
💶 Better-value standout
🍀 Classic Dublin feel
📍Address: 42 Lower Ormond Quay, Dublin 1
🌐Website: thewoollenmills.com
🕒Hours: Mon–Fri from 12, Sat–Sun from 11
🚶Distance from hotel: about 15–18 min walk
🅿️Parking: city-centre street/garage parking, not worth driving from your hotel
🍴Menu highlights: fish and chips, Woollen Mills burger, Irish fry, pork belly hash
This is the right kind of Dublin pick for you. Historic building, great location, recognizably Irish food, and normal-person menu pricing instead of tasting-menu nonsense.

Waterford · May 7–8

For one night in Waterford, the smart move is someplace flexible, local, and not overly precious.

Momo Restaurant
💶 Better-value standout
🥗 Broad menu, easy fit
📍Address: 47 Patrick Street, Waterford, X91 DX49
🌐Website: momorestaurant.ie
🕒Hours: Tue–Thu 12–9, Fri–Sat 12–10, Sun 1–8, closed Mon
🚶Distance from hotel: about 10–12 min walk
🅿️Parking: use central Waterford public parking, then walk
🍴Menu highlights: locally sourced meat, fish, vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free and dairy-free options
This is a better fit than a formal occasion restaurant. It is local, broad, fresh, and gives you room to choose what sounds good that night instead of forcing a “special dinner” mood.

Cork · May 8–11

Cork deserves food that feels like Cork, not a generic upscale plate. This is the most reliable all-around choice.

Market Lane
💶 Strong value for quality
🍀 Local, seasonal, lively
📍Address: 5 & 6 Oliver Plunkett Street, Cork
🌐Website: marketlane.ie
🕒Hours: check current day online before going, but it serves lunch and dinner daily
🚶Distance from hotel: about 15–18 min walk
🅿️Parking: nearby city-centre garages, but walking from the Metropole is easier
🍴Menu highlights: fish, salads, meat, sandwiches, and broad coeliac-friendly choices
This is the sweet spot. It is well-regarded, polished enough to feel like a real pick, but not so high-end that it becomes a production. Good food, city energy, normal pricing.

Dingle · May 11–14

Dingle is where overthinking food is a mistake. Go for the place that feels fresh, local, and honest.

The Fish Box
🐟 Fresh catch, no showboating
🍀 Dingle without fuss
📍Address: Upper Green Street, Dingle
🌐Website: thefishboxdingle.com
🕒Hours: seasonal, check socials/site before going
🚶Distance from hotel: about 8 min walk
🅿️Parking: street parking in town, but easiest on foot from your stay
🍴Menu highlights: local seafood, daily specials, fish and chips, simple fresh catches
This is much more your speed than a “special occasion” seafood room. It is exactly the kind of place where good fish becomes the memory, not the bill.

Galway · May 14–16

Galway has plenty of polished options, but the smarter call is one of the old reliable local institutions.

McDonagh’s
🐟 Galway institution
💶 Worth it without overspending
📍Address: 22 Quay Street, Galway
🌐Website: mcdonaghs.net
🕒Hours: Mon–Sat 12–10, Sun 1–9
🚗Distance from hotel: about 8–10 min drive from Nox Hotel
🅿️Parking: use city-centre parking and walk into the Latin Quarter
🍴Menu highlights: fish and chip bar, seafood restaurant, classic Galway seafood stop
This is the kind of place that earns its reputation by doing one thing well for a long time. It feels more grounded and more useful for your trip than paying extra for a trendy room.

Derry · May 16–18

Derry is more about warmth and atmosphere than chasing fine dining. This is the practical winner.

Fitzroy’s Bistro
🍺 Family-run local pick
🥘 Comfort-food win
📍Address: 2–4 Bridge Street, Derry, BT48 6JZ
🌐Website: fitzroysrestaurant.com
🕒Hours: check booking page for the day, typically lunch through evening service
🚶Distance from hotel: about 10 min walk
🅿️Parking: leave the car at the hotel and walk into the walled city
🍴Menu highlights: seafood chowder, locally sourced lamb shank, cocktails and full bar
This is exactly the right Derry move. Local, established, family-run, easy to walk to, and no fake grandeur. It should feel like a proper evening out, not homework.

Dublin final stay · May 18–21

For Ballsbridge, the best answer is a strong gastropub that still feels like Dublin and does not demand Michelin-level commitment.

The Old Spot
🍺 Great pub-food pick
🚶 Easy from Pembroke
📍Address: 14 Bath Avenue, Sandymount, Dublin 4
🌐Website: theoldspot.ie
🕒Hours: Mon–Fri lunch 12–3, dinner 5–9:30; Sat lunch 12:30–3, dinner 5–9:30; Sun roast 12:30–3 and 5–8
🚶Distance from hotel: about 15 min walk
🅿️Parking: street parking nearby, but walking from your guest suite is easiest
🍴Menu highlights: gastropub mains, Sunday roast, whiskey list, craft beer
This is how to finish Dublin without drifting into expensive-food theater. It still feels like a destination, but in a grounded, enjoyable, sit-down-and-relax way.
Tip: Check hours on the day you go and book ahead where helpful, especially for weekend evenings.
