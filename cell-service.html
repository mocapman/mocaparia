Ireland Connectivity Strategy: May 5 – May 21

Primary Goal: Use the "Strict Airplane Mode" method to rely on Wi-Fi and offline tools, keeping costs near $0.

1. The AT&T Safety Net

  • Plan: Add the International Day Pass (IDP) to your account before departure.
  • Cost: $0 if unused; $12/day if triggered.
  • Benefit: Prevents massive "pay-per-use" fees ($2.05/MB) if you must use data in an emergency.

2. The "Strict Airplane Mode" Protocol

  • Activation: Turn Airplane Mode ON before landing and keep it on for the duration of the trip.
  • Wi-Fi Use: Manually toggle Wi-Fi ON while in Airplane Mode to connect to hotel/cafe networks.
  • Wi-Fi Calling: Ensure "Wi-Fi Calling" is enabled in Settings before leaving the US to call/text US numbers for free over Wi-Fi.

3. Data Leak Prevention (Google Power User Edition)

  • Google Photos: Toggle "Use mobile data to back up" to OFF.
  • Wi-Fi Assist: In iPhone Cellular Settings, scroll to the bottom and toggle OFF "Wi-Fi Assist" to prevent accidental data usage.
  • Mail: Set "Fetch New Data" to Manual to stop background pings.
  • Auto-Update: Set Google Play/App Store and Offline Maps to "Over Wi-Fi only."

4. Navigation & Offline Prep

  • Download Now: Select "Entire Ireland" in Google Maps Offline.
  • Backup: Download local areas in Apple Maps as a secondary resource.
  • Local Apps: Install FREE NOW (Taxis) and Leap Top-up (Transport) while still in the US.

Reminder: If you toggle Airplane Mode OFF to check a text or map, you trigger a 24-hour IDP window ($12).
